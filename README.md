# SELENIA Lunar Mission Design

SELENIA is an academic mission and system design project developed within the *Space Missions and Systems Design* course at Politecnico di Torino.

The project focuses on the preliminary design of a lunar SmallSat mission aimed at characterizing the lunar radiation environment and monitoring the biological response of selected microorganisms under deep-space conditions.

## Project Scope

The study covers the early-phase design of a lunar mission, including both mission-level and system-level analyses. The work follows a structured engineering approach, starting from stakeholder needs and mission objectives and progressing toward requirements definition, Concept of Operations, mission architecture, subsystem design, risk assessment and verification planning.

## Main Activities

- Stakeholder needs analysis
- Mission statement and objectives definition
- Science Traceability Matrix development
- Mission requirements definition
- Concept of Operations
- Lunar mission architecture trade-off
- Orbit selection and trajectory analysis
- Station-keeping and disposal strategy
- Delta-V budget estimation
- System requirements definition
- Spacecraft operational modes
- System architecture and subsystem sizing
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategy
- AIV planning
- MBSE workflow implementation

## Technical Areas
